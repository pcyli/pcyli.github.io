Chia Te - Pineapple Cake
This is the extremely well known version of the pineapple cake from Taiwan. Chia Te bakery does the original recipe very well and has a well deserved reputation. The bakery makes a variety of these pineapple cakes from the original (shown here), cherry pineapple, longan, pineapple and egg yolk amongst others. The original variant of the "pineapple cake" also includes wax gourd. You might have seen it during Lunar New Years when it's candied. This was done because back in the day the canning and processing of pineapple caused the pastries to taste bad. Sugar and wax gourd were introduced to make it more palatable.
You can only get these in specific locations and primarily at their 1 bakery store in Taipei. Chikan Tower Kuixing Brush
This particular pencil is from Chikan Tower in Tainan. The tower has a storied history, first as a Dutch fort named Fort Provintia. It was rebuilt several times and once acted as the capital building of the Taiwan island during Dutch colonization. The top level has a commanding view of the area and is the oldest building in the region. The complex itself contains a number of different sites excluding the actual fort. A pavilion, the Haisheng (Ocean god) temple and the site of a old academy turned hospital during the Japanese occupation.
The pencil itself is has the name of Chikan Tower (赤崁樓) and Kuixing Brush (魁星筆) written on the side of it. In the past, Chikan Tower was used to worship the Dragon King of the Four Seas and Mazu. In particular Kuixing is represented by the 4 stars in the constellation of the Big Dipper that forms the dipper. He is also the god of exams and one of the Five Gods of Literature! In order to get this pencil, a vistor has to ask a question to Kuixing and have him answer in the affirmative by reading the jiao bei. A bit esoteric but a fun experience!
